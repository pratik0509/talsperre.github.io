Traffic Violations in Metropolitan Cities (Ahmedabad)

Aanshul Sadaria, Himanshu Bhatia, Kanay Gupta, Pratik Jain, Shashank Srikanth

Abstract

The aim of the project is to find interesting patterns in the e-challan generation and payment. This project was done as a part of Course Big Data and Policing, taught at IIIT, Hyderabad by Professor PK. For our study we chose to study the e-challans generated by the Ahmedabad City Police Department for Traffic violations.

Data Collection

We had collected the unpaid e-challan data from Ahmedabad website by brute forcing over all the possible plate numbers starting from GJ01**** using Selenium. To make our study more interesting we made another attempt at scraping the unpaid challans by only querying those vehicle numbers which we had previously collected. The data we receive consists of date of issuance of challan, notice number, vehicle number, location, fine amount and violation type.

Observations

Collected 1.6M data with approx 700K unique vehicle numbers.

Petty violations were most common ones like - Red Light violations were highest, not wearing helmet, improper parking violations, etc.

Recidivism was pretty common for unpaid challans.
